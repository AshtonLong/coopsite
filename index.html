01 // ABSTRACT

Government operations are often perceived as slow and manual. My objective was to shatter that perception.

During the Summer 2025 term, I served as an Operations Control Analyst for the Ontario Public Service (OPS), specifically within the Ministry of Public and Business Service Delivery and Procurement. Based at the data center in Guelph, I analyzed systems to discover bottlenecks and developed internal tooling to speed up operations.

The mission was clear: Eliminate toil, enhance reliability, and significantly reduce downtime.

QUERY // 001

"Give an overview of your job or project. Highlight the most interesting or unique aspects of your job. What skills did you need for the job? Did you learn them in class or on the job?"

FREEDOM & PHILOSOPHY

The most interesting part was certainly the freedom given to the coop students. We got to explore topics we found interesting (C++) for example. And we got to build real, useful tools.

They had the philosophy that younger, newer eyes can see solutions that older people in the industry might miss.

THE MANDATE

My job was automating the grunt work, upkeeping the scripts to ensure the automations continue working as intended, and self-learning to develop better things in the future.

SKILL ACQUISITION

To be proficient on the job, I had to have a good understanding of the Microsoft ecosystem: Windows, Excel, Word, Power BI, Fabric and more.

I learnt Power BI on the job, but have tinkered with all before on my freetime, allowing me to further refine my knowledge on the job.

GROWTH VECTORS

01. INTERNAL TOOLING (AI & WEB)

My primary objective was to eliminate bottlenecks in ticket resolution. I developed a modern web app using AI embeddings and clustering to identify similar past problems and route tickets effectively, replacing legacy systems.

02. FULL STACK ARSENAL

Gained deep confidence in HTML, CSS, JS, and Python by building real tools rather than passive learning. This project solidified my ability to deliver end-to-end solutions.

03. C++ PROFICIENCY

Accountability for my own growth led to a self-directed deep dive into C++. Through textbooks and experimentation during downtime, I added low-level systems programming to my skillset.

04. REFLECTION & FUTURE

I targeted AI integration to align with industry trends. Between this and hands-on Mainframe labs, I've validated that active building is the most effective way to master complex domains.
